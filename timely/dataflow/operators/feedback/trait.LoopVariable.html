Creates a Stream and a Handle to later bind the source of that Stream.

The resulting Stream will have its data defined by a future call to connect_loop with its Handle passed as an argument. Data will be passed through the stream will have their timestampsas advanced by summary, and will be dropped if the result exceeds limit.

Examples

use timely::dataflow::operators::{LoopVariable, ConnectLoop, ToStream, Concat, Inspect};

timely::example(|scope| {
    // circulate 0..10 for 100 iterations.
    let (handle, cycle) = scope.loop_variable(100, 1);
    (0..10).to_stream(scope)
           .concat(&cycle)
           .inspect(|x| println!("seen: {:?}", x))
           .connect_loop(handle);
});
